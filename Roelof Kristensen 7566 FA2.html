Hisory of coffee

The origin of coffee date back to the 600 A.D. There are several legends about the origins of coffee, one of wich
states that in eastern Africa, a sheperd called Kaldi observed that his goats where overactive when they ate
cherry-red berries from a shrub. He tried to eat some of the berries, and found himself invigorated too. Soon,
news spread about the stimulating affects of these berries, and monks started sing the berries to stay awake
during their long hours of prayer.

From Eastern Africa, coffee was transported to Arabia and then to Turkey, where they roasted the beans
and boiled them and gave rise to a crude of coffee that we use today. Soon, coffee spread to Europe,
America, and other countries, and now it is one of the most popular beverages enjoyed by multitude of
people all over the world.
